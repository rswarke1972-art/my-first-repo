Design Issues

Design Issues in database design refer to important decisions that must be taken while creating an ER model and database schema. Proper design ensures efficiency and consistency.

Major Design Issues

1. Entity vs Attribute

  • Decide whether a concept should be an entity or an attribute

2. Entity vs Relationship

  • Determine if a concept should be represented as entity or relationship

3. Binary vs N-ary Relationships

  • Choose correct relationship type

4. Placement of Attributes

  • Decide where attributes should belong

5. Use of Weak Entities

  • Identify when weak entities are required

Importance

  • Improves database efficiency
  • Reduces redundancy
  • Ensures data consistency

Conclusion

Careful handling of design issues leads to a well-structured and efficient database system.
