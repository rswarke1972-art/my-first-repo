Serializability: Conflict and View

Serializability ensures that a non-serial schedule produces the same result as a serial schedule. It guarantees consistency during concurrent execution.

1. Conflict Serializability

  • Based on conflicting operations
  • Two operations conflict if:
    • They belong to different transactions
    • They access same data item
    • At least one operation is write
  • Can be tested using precedence graph

2. View Serializability

  • Based on view equivalence
  • Schedules are equivalent if:
    • Same initial reads
    • Same final writes
  • More general than conflict serializability

Importance

  • Ensures correctness in concurrent execution
  • Maintains data consistency

Conclusion

Serializability guarantees that concurrent transactions behave like serial execution.
