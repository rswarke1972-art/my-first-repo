Recoverable and Non-recoverable Schedules

Schedules are classified based on whether they allow safe recovery from transaction failures.

1. Recoverable Schedule

  • If T2 reads data written by T1, then T2 commits only after T1 commits
  • Ensures safe recovery

Example

T1: Write(A) → Commit T2: Read(A) → Commit

2. Non-recoverable Schedule

  • T2 commits before T1 commits
  • If T1 aborts, recovery becomes impossible

Example

T1: Write(A) T2: Read(A) → Commit T1: Abort

Importance

  • Recoverable schedules prevent inconsistencies

Conclusion

Recoverable schedules ensure safe transaction execution, while non-recoverable schedules may lead to data inconsistency.
