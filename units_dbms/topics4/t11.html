Transaction Management

Transaction Management refers to the techniques used by DBMS to ensure that transactions are executed reliably and consistently.

Objectives of Transaction Management

  • Maintain database consistency
  • Handle system failures
  • Control concurrent access

Components of Transaction Management

  • Concurrency Control
  • Recovery Management

Responsibilities

  • Ensure atomic execution
  • Maintain isolation between transactions
  • Guarantee durability of committed data

Conclusion

Transaction Management ensures safe and reliable execution of database operations.
