Chains and Anti-Chains

Chains and Anti-Chains are concepts used in partially ordered sets (posets).

Chain

  • A subset of a poset where every pair of elements is comparable
  • Elements follow a linear order

Example

If A = {1, 2, 3} with ≤ relation, then {1, 2, 3} is a chain.

Anti-Chain

  • A subset of a poset where no two elements are comparable

Example

In divisibility relation on {2, 3, 5}, none divides another → forms an anti-chain.

Conclusion

Chains represent ordered subsets, while anti-chains represent incomparable elements.
