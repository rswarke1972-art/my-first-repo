Introduction to Relations

A relation is a connection between elements of two sets. It is defined as a subset of the Cartesian product of two sets.

Definition

  • If A and B are sets, a relation R from A to B is a subset of A × B

Example

A = {1, 2} B = {a, b}

A × B = {(1,a), (1,b), (2,a), (2,b)}

R = {(1,a), (2,b)}

Domain and Range

  • Domain: First elements of ordered pairs
  • Range: Second elements of ordered pairs

Conclusion

Relations define associations between elements of different sets.
