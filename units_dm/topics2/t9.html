Lattices

A lattice is a special type of partially ordered set in which every pair of elements has both a greatest lower bound (meet) and a least upper bound (join).

Definitions

  • Join (a ∨ b): Least upper bound
  • Meet (a ∧ b): Greatest lower bound

Properties

  • Commutative
  • Associative
  • Absorption Law

Example

The set of divisors of a number with divisibility relation forms a lattice.

Conclusion

Lattices combine order theory with algebraic structure.
