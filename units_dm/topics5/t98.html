Rings

A Ring is an algebraic structure consisting of a set with two binary operations, usually addition and multiplication.

Properties

  • (R, +) forms an abelian group
  • Multiplication is associative
  • Distributive laws hold: a(b + c) = ab + ac (a + b)c = ac + bc

Example

Set of integers (Z) with + and ×

Types of Rings

  • Commutative Ring
  • Ring with Identity

Conclusion

Rings combine two operations with specific algebraic properties.
