Binomial Coefficients

Binomial coefficients represent the number of ways to choose r objects from n objects. They are denoted as nCr or C(n, r).

Formula

nCr = n! / [r! (n - r)!]

Binomial Theorem

(a + b)^n = Σ (nCr) a^(n-r) b^r

Example

(1 + x)^3 = 1 + 3x + 3x² + x³

Properties

  • nC0 = 1
  • nCn = 1
  • nCr = nC(n-r)

Conclusion

Binomial coefficients are used in combinatorics and algebraic expansions.
