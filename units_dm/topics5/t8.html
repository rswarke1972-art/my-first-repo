Inclusion-Exclusion Principle

The Inclusion-Exclusion Principle is used to count the number of elements in the union of overlapping sets without double counting.

For Two Sets

|A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| − |A ∩ B|

For Three Sets

|A ∪ B ∪ C| = |A| + |B| + |C| − |A ∩ B| − |A ∩ C| − |B ∩ C| + |A ∩ B ∩ C|

Purpose

  • Avoid overcounting
  • Handle overlapping groups

Conclusion

Inclusion-Exclusion ensures accurate counting in overlapping cases.
