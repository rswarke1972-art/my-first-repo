Homomorphism

A Homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between two algebraic systems.

Definition

  • Let (G, *) and (H, •) be groups
  • A function f: G → H is a homomorphism if
  • f(a * b) = f(a) • f(b)

Properties

  • Identity is preserved
  • Inverse is preserved

Example

f(x) = 2x from (Z, +) to (Z, +) is a homomorphism.

Conclusion

Homomorphisms maintain the algebraic structure between systems.
