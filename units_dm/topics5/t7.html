Pigeonhole Principle

The Pigeonhole Principle states that if more objects are placed into fewer boxes, at least one box must contain more than one object.

Basic Form

  • If n objects are placed into m boxes and n > m, then at least one box contains at least two objects.

General Form

If n objects are placed into m boxes, then at least one box contains at least ⌈n/m⌉ objects.

Example

If 13 people are in a room, at least two share the same birth month.

Conclusion

The Pigeonhole Principle guarantees repetition when items exceed containers.
