Countable and Uncountable Sets

Sets are classified as countable or uncountable based on whether their elements can be listed in a sequence.

Countable Set

  • Elements can be listed in sequence
  • Can form one-to-one correspondence with natural numbers
  • Example: Natural numbers, Integers

Uncountable Set

  • Elements cannot be listed completely
  • No one-to-one correspondence with natural numbers
  • Example: Real numbers

Finite and Infinite Countable Sets

  • Finite sets are countable
  • Infinite sets like natural numbers are also countable

Conclusion

Countable sets can be listed, while uncountable sets are too large to be listed completely.
