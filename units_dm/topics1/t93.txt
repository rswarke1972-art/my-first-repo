<h2>Bounded and Unbounded Sets</h2>

<p>
A set is called bounded if all its elements lie
within some fixed limit. Otherwise, it is unbounded.
</p>

<h3>Bounded Set</h3>
<ul>
    <li>All elements lie between certain values</li>
    <li>Has upper and lower bounds</li>
    <li>Example: A = {x | 1 ≤ x ≤ 5}</li>
</ul>

<h3>Unbounded Set</h3>
<ul>
    <li>No fixed upper or lower limit</li>
    <li>Example: Natural numbers {1, 2, 3, ...}</li>
</ul>

<h3>Upper and Lower Bound</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Upper Bound: Greatest possible value</li>
    <li>Lower Bound: Smallest possible value</li>
</ul>

<h3>Conclusion</h3>
<p>
Bounded sets have limits,
while unbounded sets extend infinitely.
</p>
