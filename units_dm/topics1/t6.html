Strong Mathematical Induction

Strong Mathematical Induction is a variation of mathematical induction where the inductive step assumes the statement is true for all values up to k to prove it for k + 1.

Steps of Strong Induction

1. Basis Step

  • Prove statement for initial value (n = 1)

2. Inductive Hypothesis

  • Assume statement is true for all values from 1 to k

3. Inductive Step

  • Prove statement is true for k + 1

Difference from Simple Induction

  • Simple induction assumes true for only k
  • Strong induction assumes true for all values ≤ k

Conclusion

Strong induction is useful for proofs that depend on multiple previous cases.
