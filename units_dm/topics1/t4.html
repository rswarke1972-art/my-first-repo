Translating English Sentences into Logic

Translating English sentences into logical expressions helps represent real-world statements using symbols and logical operators.

Steps for Translation

  • Identify simple statements (propositions)
  • Assign symbols (p, q, r)
  • Identify logical connectives

Common English Words and Their Logical Forms

  • "and" → ∧ (Conjunction)
  • "or" → ∨ (Disjunction)
  • "if...then" → → (Implication)
  • "if and only if" → ↔ (Biconditional)
  • "not" → ¬ (Negation)

Example

Statement: "If it rains, then the ground is wet."

Let p = It rains Let q = Ground is wet Logical form: p → q

Conclusion

Translating English into logic improves precision and supports formal reasoning.
