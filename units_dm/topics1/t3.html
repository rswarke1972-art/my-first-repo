Propositional Equivalences

Propositional Equivalences are logical statements that have the same truth values in all possible cases. They are used to simplify logical expressions.

Common Logical Equivalences

1. Identity Laws

  • p ∧ T ≡ p
  • p ∨ F ≡ p

2. Domination Laws

  • p ∨ T ≡ T
  • p ∧ F ≡ F

3. Double Negation

  • ¬(¬p) ≡ p

4. De Morgan’s Laws

  • ¬(p ∧ q) ≡ ¬p ∨ ¬q
  • ¬(p ∨ q) ≡ ¬p ∧ ¬q

5. Implication Law

  • p → q ≡ ¬p ∨ q

Importance

  • Simplifies logical expressions
  • Used in circuit design

Conclusion

Propositional equivalences help in transforming and simplifying logic statements.
