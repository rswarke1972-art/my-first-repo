Principle of Inclusion and Exclusion

The Principle of Inclusion and Exclusion (PIE) is used to calculate the number of elements in the union of two or more sets.

For Two Sets

|A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| − |A ∩ B|

For Three Sets

|A ∪ B ∪ C| = |A| + |B| + |C| − |A ∩ B| − |A ∩ C| − |B ∩ C| + |A ∩ B ∩ C|

Purpose

  • Avoid double counting
  • Count elements accurately

Example

If 20 students like Math, 15 like Science, 5 like both,

Total = 20 + 15 − 5 = 30

Conclusion

Inclusion and Exclusion principle helps calculate total elements in overlapping sets.
