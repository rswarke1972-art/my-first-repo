Graph Isomorphism

Two graphs are said to be isomorphic if they have the same structure even if their vertices are labeled differently.

Definition

  • There exists a one-to-one correspondence between vertices of both graphs
  • Adjacency is preserved

Conditions

  • Same number of vertices
  • Same number of edges
  • Same degree sequence

Importance

  • Used in network comparison
  • Used in chemical structure analysis

Conclusion

Graph isomorphism checks whether two graphs have identical structure.
