Logic Instructions

Logic Instructions in 8086 are used to perform logical operations such as AND, OR, NOT, and XOR. These operations are performed bit by bit on the operands.

Common Logic Instructions

1. AND

  • Performs bitwise AND operation
  • Example: AND AX, BX

2. OR

  • Performs bitwise OR operation
  • Example: OR AX, CX

3. XOR

  • Performs bitwise Exclusive OR operation
  • Example: XOR AX, DX

4. NOT

  • Performs bitwise complement
  • Example: NOT AX

5. TEST

  • Performs AND operation but does not store result
  • Only updates flags

Flags Affected

  • Zero Flag (ZF)
  • Sign Flag (SF)
  • Parity Flag (PF)

Conclusion

Logic Instructions are important for decision making, masking bits, and performing bit-level operations in 8086.
