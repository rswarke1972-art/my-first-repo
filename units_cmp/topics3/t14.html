Arithmetic Instructions

Arithmetic Instructions in 8086 are used to perform mathematical operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. These instructions affect the flag register.

Common Arithmetic Instructions

1. ADD

  • Adds source operand to destination
  • Example: ADD AX, BX

2. SUB

  • Subtracts source from destination
  • Example: SUB AX, CX

3. INC

  • Increments operand by 1
  • Example: INC AX

4. DEC

  • Decrements operand by 1
  • Example: DEC BX

5. MUL

  • Unsigned multiplication

6. DIV

  • Unsigned division

7. CMP

  • Compares two operands
  • Sets flags but does not store result

Flags Affected

  • Zero Flag (ZF)
  • Carry Flag (CF)
  • Sign Flag (SF)
  • Overflow Flag (OF)

Conclusion

Arithmetic Instructions are essential for performing mathematical calculations in 8086 and play a major role in program execution.
