Programmer's Model of 8086

The programmer's model of 8086 shows the internal registers and their functions from a programming point of view. It helps the programmer understand how data is stored and processed inside the CPU.

Main Components

1. General Purpose Registers

  • AX (Accumulator)
  • BX (Base Register)
  • CX (Count Register)
  • DX (Data Register)

2. Segment Registers

  • CS (Code Segment)
  • DS (Data Segment)
  • SS (Stack Segment)
  • ES (Extra Segment)

3. Pointer and Index Registers

  • SP (Stack Pointer)
  • BP (Base Pointer)
  • SI (Source Index)
  • DI (Destination Index)

4. Instruction Pointer

  • IP – Holds the address of next instruction

5. Flag Register

  • Contains status and control flags
  • Indicates result of arithmetic and logical operations

Conclusion

The programmer's model of 8086 provides a clear view of internal registers and their roles. It helps programmers write efficient assembly language programs.
