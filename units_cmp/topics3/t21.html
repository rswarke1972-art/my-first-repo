Other Instructions

Other Instructions in 8086 include special instructions that do not directly fall under arithmetic, logic, data movement, or control categories. These instructions perform specific utility functions.

Examples of Other Instructions

1. LEA (Load Effective Address)

  • Loads effective address into a register
  • Example: LEA AX, [BX + SI]

2. LDS (Load DS Register)

  • Loads data segment register and a general register

3. LES (Load ES Register)

  • Loads extra segment register and a general register

4. LAHF (Load AH from Flags)

  • Copies lower byte of flag register into AH

5. SAHF (Store AH into Flags)

  • Copies AH register into lower byte of flag register

Importance

  • Used in advanced programming
  • Helpful in segment handling
  • Assist in flag manipulation

Conclusion

Other Instructions provide additional support for memory addressing, segment handling, and flag operations in the 8086 microprocessor.
