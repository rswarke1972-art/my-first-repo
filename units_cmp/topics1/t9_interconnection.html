Interconnection Structure

Interconnection structure refers to the way different parts of a computer (CPU, memory, and I/O devices) are connected to communicate with each other. It determines the speed and efficiency of data transfer.

Main Types of Interconnection Structures

1. Single Bus Structure

All components are connected through a single bus. This is simple and cost-effective.

  • Low cost
  • Easy design
  • But limited speed

2. Multiple Bus Structure

Two or more buses are used for communication. It improves speed and reduces bus congestion.

  • Higher speed
  • Better performance
  • More complex design

3. Crossbar Switch Structure

Every device has its own dedicated path. It provides very high speed but is costly.

  • High speed
  • Complex and expensive

Conclusion

The interconnection structure is crucial for system performance, as it determines how quickly data can move between components.
