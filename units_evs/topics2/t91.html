Water Pollution Act

The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act was enacted in 1974 in India to prevent and control water pollution.

Main Objectives

  • Prevent and control water pollution
  • Maintain and restore water quality
  • Establish Central and State Pollution Control Boards

Key Provisions

  • Regulation of discharge of pollutants
  • Monitoring of water quality
  • Penalties for non-compliance

Conclusion

The Water Pollution Act protects water resources and public health.
