Air Pollution Act

The Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act was enacted in 1981 in India to control and reduce air pollution.

Main Objectives

  • Prevent and control air pollution
  • Maintain air quality standards
  • Establish pollution control boards

Key Provisions

  • Regulation of industrial emissions
  • Monitoring air quality
  • Penalties for violations

Conclusion

The Air Pollution Act helps protect public health and environment.
